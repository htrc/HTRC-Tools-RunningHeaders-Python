 “Well, then, how’d you come to be up at the Pint in the

 “I warn’t up at the Pint in the mornin’.” 
 
 “It’s a he.” 
 
 Several of them jumped for him and begged him not to talk 
 that way to an old man and a preacher. 
 
 “Preacher be hanged, he’s a fraud and a liar. He was up at the 
 Pint that mornin’. I live up there, don’t I? Well, I was up there, 
 and he was up there. I see him there. He come in a canoe, along 
 with Tim Collins and a boy.” 
 
 The doctor he up and says: 
 
 “Would you know the boy again if you was to see him, 
 Hines?” 
 
 “I reckon I would, but I don’t know. Why, yonder he is, now. 
 I know him perfectly easy.” 
 
 It was me he pointed at. The doctor says: 
 
 “Neighbours, I don’t know whether the new couple is frauds 
 or not; but if these two ain’t frauds, I am an idiot, that’s all. I 
 think it’s our duty to see that they don’t get away from here till 
 we’ve looked into this thing. Come along, Hines; come along, 
 the rest of you. We’ll take these fellows to the tavern and affront 
 them with t’other couple, and I reckon we’ll find out something 
 before we get through.”
