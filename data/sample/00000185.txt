 CHAPTER XXV 
 
 the news was all over the town in two minutes, and you could 
 see the people tearing down on the run, from every which way, 
 some of them putting on their coats as they come. Pretty soon 
 we was in the middle of a crowd, and the noise of the tramping 
 was like a soldier-march. The windows and door-yards was full; 
 and every minute somebody would say, over a fence: 
 
 “Is it them ?” 
 
 And somebody trotting along with the gang would answer 
 back and say: 
 
 “You bet it is.” 
 
 When we got to the house, the street in front of it was packed, 
 and the three girls was standing in the door. Mary Jane was red¬ 
 headed, but that don’t make no difference, she was most awful 
 beautiful, and her face and her eyes was all lit up hke glory, she 
 was so glad her uncles was come. The king he spread his arms, 
 and Mary Jane she jumped for them, and the hare-lip jumped 
 for the duke, and there they had it! Everybody most, leastways 
 women, cried for joy to see them meet again at last and have 
 such good times.
